We analyzed MRF data across 12 major metro areas and 10 high-volume procedure codes. The variation is staggering — and it reveals exactly where the market is failing.

## Key findings

The average price variation for identical procedures within a single metro area is 3.7×. In some markets, we found variation exceeding 10× for routine imaging and lab services.

## Methodology

Our analysis covers 50 million negotiated rate records from machine-readable files published by the top 20 payers by covered lives. We focused on 10 high-volume CPT codes across 12 major metropolitan statistical areas.

## What this means

Price transparency data is finally making the invisible visible. But seeing the variation is only the first step — the question now is what we do about it.
